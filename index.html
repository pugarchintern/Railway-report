2. Digital Monitoring System & AI Methodology

The objective of Safai AI is to transform washroom management from a reactive, manually monitored process into a proactive, AI-driven digital monitoring system. SAAFAI replaces the legacy paper register process with a completely digital workflow where every cleaning activity is recorded, verified, analyzed, and converted into actionable insights.

The Digital Workflow

1. Cleaner Login & Selection
2. Upload Before Images
3. Execute Cleaning Checklist
4. Upload After Images
5. AI Image Analysis Triggered
6. Dashboard Analytics Updated

5-Tier AI Ensemble

Rather than relying on one image, SAAFAI combines outputs from several specialized models:

  • Visual Cleanliness (96% Acc): Detects floor stains, water marks, and dirty surfaces. (Weight: 30%)
  • Safety Detection (95% Acc): Identifies wet floors, puddles, and slip hazards. (Weight: 15%)
  • Garbage Detection (97% Acc): Flags overflowing bins and waste accumulation.
  • Broken Fixtures (94% Acc): Analyzes structural damage to doors, mirrors, and taps.
  • Consumables (98% Acc): Validates availability of soap and tissue paper. (Weight: 15%)

3. Physical Infrastructure Condition Assessment

In addition to evaluating cleanliness standards and operational sanitation practices, a comprehensive assessment of the physical washroom infrastructure was undertaken as part of the baseline survey. The objective of this exercise was to identify structural, plumbing, and asset-related deficiencies that may adversely impact passenger experience, safety, accessibility, and overall facility functionality.

The assessment focused on the condition of floor and wall finishes, sanitary fixtures, plumbing systems, drainage arrangements, partitions, utility fittings, and other passenger-facing assets. While such observations are independent of routine cleaning performance, they play a critical role in determining the perceived quality and usability of public sanitation facilities.

The infrastructure review identified multiple maintenance-related concerns requiring targeted civil, plumbing, and repair interventions. Addressing these issues alongside ongoing sanitation initiatives will significantly enhance facility reliability, passenger satisfaction, and long-term asset sustainability.

Deep Data Insights

  • Critical Collapse — Kamthee Station Beside Entry & Last One (Wk 7–9): Both high-traffic platform washrooms maintained average scores (6.43–6.93) through Weeks 1–6 but suffered a severe quality collapse from Week 7 onwards. Kamthee beside entry plummeted from 6.38 (Wk 6) to 3.27 (Wk 9), and Kamthee last one dropped to 3.10 (Wk 9) — both entering the Poor zone and requiring immediate corrective action and increased cleaning frequency.
  • Consistent Excellence — Pannel Office 1st Floor & Ss Office Washroom: These two washrooms are the strongest performers across the entire 9-week cycle. Pannel Office maintained scores between 8.10–8.80 (all Excellent/Good), peaking at 8.80 in Week 7. Ss Office similarly held between 7.40–8.75, with only Weeks 8–9 dipping slightly to 7.40–7.50 (Good). Controlled access and disciplined cleaner routines are the key differentiating factors.
  • Recovery Signal — HandiCap Kamthee Railway Station (Wk 7–9): The only washroom to show a sustained improvement trajectory. After holding in the Average band (6.44–6.56) across Weeks 1–6, it broke into the Good zone in Week 7 (7.47) and continued rising to 7.80 in Week 9 — suggesting effective corrective intervention and increased staff accountability from Week 7 onward.
  • Mid-Period Dip & Recovery — Rpf Staff Washroom: Rpf Staff peaked at 8.16 in Week 3 and held Good scores through Week 7 (7.50–8.10). A notable dip occurred in Week 8 (6.76 — Average zone), but the washroom recovered to 7.50 in Week 9, indicating the performance issue was temporary rather than structural.
  • Early Improvement Plateau — Booking Staff Office Washroom (Wk 1–5): This washroom started in the Average zone (6.85 in Wk 1), rapidly improved to 7.70 by Week 2 (Good), then gradually tapered to 7.40 by Week 5. No data is available for Weeks 6–9, indicating monitoring gaps that should be addressed to ensure continued compliance tracking.
